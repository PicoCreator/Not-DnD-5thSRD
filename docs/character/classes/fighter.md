The Fighter is a stalwart and courageous warrior, ever ready to face any foe with courage and strength. With their martial prowess, they are able to take on any challenge that comes their way.

At each level, the Fighter gains proficiency bonus, features, and other abilities.

At 1st level, the Fighter gains a Fighting Style, as well as the ability to use Second Wind.

At 2nd level, the Fighter gains the ability to use Action Surge once.

At 3rd level, the Fighter chooses a Martial Archetype.

At 4th level, the Fighter gains the ability to improve one of their Ability Scores.

At 5th level, the Fighter gains the ability to make an Extra Attack.

At 6th level, the Fighter gains the ability to improve one of their Ability Scores.

At 7th level, the Fighter gains a feature from their Martial Archetype.

At 8th level, the Fighter gains the ability to improve one of their Ability Scores.

At 9th level, the Fighter gains the ability to use Indomitable once.

At 10th level, the Fighter gains a feature from their Martial Archetype.

At 11th level, the Fighter gains the ability to make two Extra Attacks.

At 12th level, the Fighter gains the ability to improve one of their Ability Scores.

At 13th level, the Fighter gains the ability to use Indomitable twice.

At 14th level, the Fighter gains the ability to improve one of their Ability Scores.

At 15th level, the Fighter gains a feature from their Martial Archetype.

At 16th level, the Fighter gains the ability to improve one of their Ability Scores.

At 17th level, the Fighter gains the ability to use Action Surge twice, as well as the ability to use Indomitable three times.

At 18th level, the Fighter gains a feature from their Martial Archetype.

At 19th level, the Fighter gains the ability to improve one of their Ability Scores.

At 20th level, the Fighter gains the ability to make three Extra Attacks.

The Fighter also gains Hit Points, Proficiencies, Equipment, and Fighting Style.

Hit Points are determined by rolling a 1d10 for each Fighter level, plus the Fighter's Constitution modifier.

The Fighter is proficient in all armor, shields, simple weapons, and martial weapons. They are not proficient in any tools. The Fighter is proficient in Strength and Constitution Saving Throws, and can choose two skills from Acrobatics, Animal Handling, Athletics, History, Insight, Intimidation, Perception, and Survival.

The Fighter starts with the following equipment: (a) chain mail or (b) leather armor, longbow, and 20 arrows; (a) a martial weapon and a shield or (b) two martial weapons; (a) a light crossbow and 20 bolts or (b) two handaxes; (a) a dungeoneer's pack or (b) and explorer's pack.

The Fighter can choose one of the following Fighting Styles: Archery, Defense, Dueling, Great Weapon Fighting, Protection, or Two-Weapon Fighting.

### Second Wind
- - -
You have a limited reservoir of vigor that you can draw upon to protect yourself from harm. On your turn, you can use a bonus action to regain hit points equal to 1d10 + your fighter level. Once you have used this feature, you must take a short or long rest before you can use it again.

### Action Surge 
- - -
Beginning at 2nd level, you can push yourself beyond your normal limits for a moment. On your turn, you can take one additional action on top of your regular action and a possible bonus action. Once you have used this feature, you must take a short or long rest before you can use it again. Starting at 17th level, you can use it twice before a rest, but only once on the same turn. 

### Martial Archetype 
- - -
At 3rd level, you choose an archetype that you strive to emulate in your combat styles and techniques. Choose Champion, Battle Master, or Eldritch Knight, all detailed at the end of the class description. The archetype you choose grants you features at 3rd level and again at 7th, 10th, 15th, and 18th level. 

### Ability Score Improvement 
- - -
When you reach 4th level, and again at 6th, 8th, 12th, 14th, 16th, and 19th level, you can increase one ability score of your choice by 2, or you can increase two ability scores of your choice by 1. As normal, you can't increase an ability score above 20 using this feature. 

### Extra Attack 
- - -
Beginning at 5th level, you can attack twice, instead of once, whenever you take the Attack action on your turn. The number of attacks increases to three when you reach 11th level in this class and to four when you reach 20th level in this class. 

### Indomitable 
- - -
Beginning at 9th level, you can reroll a saving throw that you fail. If you do so, you must use the new roll, and you can’t use this feature again until you have taken a long rest. You can use this feature twice between long rests starting at 13th level and three times between long rests starting at 17th level.

## Martial Archetypes 
Different fighters choose different approaches to perfecting their fighting prowess. The martial archetype you choose to emulate reflects your approach. 

### Champion 
- - -
The archetypal Champion focuses on the development of raw physical power honed to deadly perfection. Those who model themselves on this archetype combine rigorous training with physical excellence to deal devastating blows. 

#### Improved Critical 
Beginning when you choose this archetype at 3rd level, your weapon attacks score a critical hit on a roll of 19 or 20. 

#### Remarkable Athlete 
Starting at 7th level, you can add half your proficiency bonus (round up) to any Strength, Dexterity, or Constitution check you make that doesn’t already use your proficiency bonus. In addition, when you make a running long jump, the distance you can cover increases by a number of feet equal to your Strength modifier. 

#### Additional Fighting Style 
At 10th level, you can choose a second option from the Fighting Style class feature.

#### Superior Critical
Starting at 15th level, your weapon attacks score a critical hit on a roll of 18–20. 

#### Survivor 
At 18th level, you attain the pinnacle of resilience in battle. At the start of each of your turns, you regain hit points equal to 5 + your Constitution modifier if you have no more than half of your hit points left. You don’t gain this benefit if you have 0 hit points.
